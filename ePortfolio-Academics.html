About Me

My Personal and Academic Journey

Who I Am.

Traditional is not a word I would use to describe myself, nor is it a word that I would use to describe my educational experience. In my sophomore year of college, I was selected to join the Keleher Research Group to further understand the chemical mechanical planarization (C.M.P.) process. My project's role was to understand the mechanisms behind surface-energy interactions between wafers and colloidal dispersions (slurry) and propose mechanistic models for said interactions. Although I found myself invigorated with the data collection and presentation processes, I knew chemical research and development was not for me.

Working in a consulting firm this summer allowed me to use my data analytics techniques better. As part of the research I was tasked with at the consulting firm, I had to access the City of Chicago data portal to compile relevant information related to building permits and business licenses to assist our clients. Methods of data collection and storage were outdated, and I helped implement new systems for better organization and created a user-friendly database for project navigation. I relished in the process of acquiring, cleaning, and using the data, which further ignited my interest in the data science field.

After discovering my passion for data science and all the topics it encompassed, I registered for programming coursework and became skilled in Python, R, SQL, statistical learning, and modeling, along with cloud computing and machine learning. My senior-year capstone project focused on using liquid crystal (L.C.) sensor technology to combat the growing problem of laser attacks on private, commercial, and federal airlines. Using libraries like Matplotlib and Pandas in Python, I was able to extrapolate and statistically analyze data from the Federal Administration Aviation (F.A.A.) and J.M.P. to further model and manipulate data to draw correlations between optical properties in lasers to liquid crystals used for light polarization. Using HTML and CSS, I published a webpage summarizing my project results and my GitHub to allow further data discussion and critiquing. This experience has taught me how to pose and answer my research questions effectively and how to defend these findings by incorporating a false understanding, controls, and event study in my analysis.

My love and interest in this topic are reflected in my academic success, as I discovered who I was as a person and scientist, I was able to better understand and rationalize my coursework material. Apart from academics and research, I’ve also taken my academic learning to a mentoring level. Since 2018, I have been an active member in scientific community outreach around the Chicagoland area, involved in events ranging from judging elementary/middle school science fairs to performing chemistry demonstrations to help young children. I extended my mentoring work to my school where I worked as a General Chemistry II Lab research mentor. Furthermore, I immerse myself in enriching global experiences such as using my Spanish to communicate with people from a variety of upbringings and encourage them by telling them my story in the S.T.E.M. field to push for greater diversity and more inclusivity within the data science community.
